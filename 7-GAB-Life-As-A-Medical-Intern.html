Life As A Medical Intern:

The First Step In Becoming A Complete Doctor!

Congratulations, you just completed a good number of years studying, living, and breathing medicine. It was indeed a roller-coaster ride with so many medical courses and those time-consuming lab sessions. Now you can finally call yourself a Doctor. But, the journey to earn that full-fledged medical license has just begun. At this point, you will have to complete your medical internship first, which is the first step to experience doctor hood-where you will get the practical overview on your medical study of so many years. As a medical intern, you will develop knowledge, skill, and bedside manner, which will give you a better understanding of how to become a doctor.

As soon as you finish your medical degree, you will have that eagerness to get started and to be called as the real doctor. But, this will require you to go through your internship in order to have the deep understanding of the medicine study. During an internship, you will be trained in a hands-on, intensive medical environment, carving you to be the best medical professional. This will be the real test of your years of medical education. So, here’s an overview of the life of a medical intern. It might look scary in the beginning, but will definitely get better as you move on!

Since it will be your first internship after studying medicine, so you will wake up early with the goal of having a healthy breakfast and a bit of warm up session, which will be followed by your on-time reporting to the hospital. Then you’ll check up on overnight admits, have morning rounds for some hours, visit the patients on your list to check their progress, will work closely with senior doctors to understand how everything works and how to make diagnosis, figure out the symptoms, and hold discussions with your fellow doctors regarding what all have you learnt and observed till now. In this way, your entire internship will be composed of learning, learning, and just learning.

Having completed your medical study in so many medical courses calls for a serious internship and if you do that well, you will definitely meet the expectations of a quality doctor. Your internship time is a precious phase of medical education, where you are fully-immersed into learning medicine. Therefore, as a medical intern, expect your maximum time being spent in analyzing those lab tests, understanding the patients’ conditions, studying symptoms and treatments, holding open-table sessions to learn from others’ experiences and a lot more. And, not to forget, you will have to invest a lot of time in the starting so expect long hours.

In addition to all these practices, a medical intern is subjected to an all-around hospital exposure, where you will rotate through a number of departments in order to have an understanding of each and every area of the hospital and the various medical specialties and subspecialties they deal in. All of this will eventually help you in finding your niche and perfecting your medical skills for the same.

Doing it in this way is sure to make you feel that you made a difference in the lives of a patient thus will boost your confidence to work towards becoming a successful doctor.
